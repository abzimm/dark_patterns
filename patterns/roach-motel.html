roach motel

WHEN SIGNING up for an account or service, it’s not immediately obvious to users that deleting that account might be more difficult. The “roach motel” pattern makes it easy to become a subscriber or user of something, but forces people to go through a complicated process in order to delete/log-out/unsubscribe.

For example, Facebook requires going through multiple tabs, settings, dialogue boxes, and confirmations to delete an account. Sometimes calling customer service is also required when attempting to dislodge from a digital product.
