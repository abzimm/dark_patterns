misleading choice

MISLEADING CHOICES are deliberate UI designs that nudge the user toward the choice the company wants them to make. Checkboxes allowing for more permissions shared are often checked by default. The “no” option is often grayed out or made less visually appealing.

When someone pays less attention, they might accidentally agree to something they didn’t mean to. It can also be easy to assume that the easiest path is the correct one, or that default settings are meant to be beneficial.
